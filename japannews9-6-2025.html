Japan — News (6 Sep 2025)

Top highlights: Imperial ceremony, diplomatic visit, typhoon update, and regional strategic relations.

1) Prince Hisahito’s Coming-of-Age Ceremony

Prince Hisahito, second in line to Japan’s throne, was officially recognized as an adult during a traditional imperial ceremony marking his 19th birthday. The event sparked renewed debate over Japan’s male-only succession law.

Sources: News agency reports & international coverage.

2) Defense Minister’s Visit to South Korea (First in 10 Years)

Japan’s Defense Minister Gen Nakatani began a three-day official visit to South Korea, meeting his counterpart to discuss bilateral security and defense cooperation. This marks the first such visit in a decade.

Source: Jiji Press / official statements.

3) Typhoon Update — (Typhoon No. 15)

Typhoon No. 15 made landfall in Wakayama and is moving toward the Kanto region. Authorities issued warnings for affected areas, and local media reported disruptions including flight cancellations.

Source: Local weather updates & media reports.

4) Japan–Australia Relations Strengthened

Japan and Australia released a joint statement to further enhance strategic and security cooperation in the Indo-Pacific region, amid growing regional challenges.

Source: Joint statement reports.

5) Political News — Ishiba Faction and U.S. Invitation

Reports surfaced that members of the Ishiba faction proposed inviting former U.S. President Donald Trump to Japan, stirring political debate on diplomacy and economic strategy.

Source: Asahi Shimbun reports.
